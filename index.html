The Branch of Parasitic Research

BPR is a privately-funded worldwide organizaion that was started on January 14th, 1698 by Jonas Abott. We at BPR strive to create a safe future for humanity, while also securing any additional information that can be gathered about parasites and keepers.

If you have any questions about parasites or keepers, please contact the Help Desk with your concerns. Our staff will be happy to answer any questions or concerns.

Parasite and Keeper Information

The true origin of these anomalies isn't yet known. Parasite pods are formed underground, and upon reaching fresh air, hatch into what we see today. Using spores, parasites will then lure a person closer, forcing them to be its caretaker. A parasite cannot survive without the keeper, so BPR strives to contain the keepers in a safe, guarded habitat in one of our facilities.

Both of these species are very dangerous, and come in many forms. Most keepers are mutated upon being exposed to spores. (See Public Keeper Case Logs to find out more about the mutations.) Some keepers do not have any outward mutations, and a few have been known to freely walk amongst the public, which we at the Branch of Parasitic Research perceive as threats to public safety.

Parasites are highly intelligent, using floral mimicry and spores to deceive people and animals into a false sense of safety. Most parasites drastically change the environment that they infect, though some need a specialized eye to detect and locate. Parasites are omnivores, though their diet mainly consist of living prey. If you notice strange thoughts that are out of place, or an urge to start walking to any remote location, cover your mouth and nose. Do not engage with parasite or keeper activity. If you think that you are being exposed to spores, promptly call your local BPR-PD and explain the situation. Remain focused and vigilant until help arrives. If there are others in your immediate area, warn them immediately.

Do You Suspect a Parasite or Keeper in Your Area?

  • Odd or abnormal animal activity including passiveness and aggression
  • Strange coloration and shape of vines, known as parasite tendrils
  • Unusual activity from those around you
  • Native flora suddenly changing colors, gaining or losing defining traits, and growing at an excellerated rate
  • Disappearances, brutal attacks, missing persons, or bodies found
  • Unusual smells in the outside air.

Reporting a Parasite or Keeper

If you suspect that there is a parasite in your area, please go to Concerns > Reports > Report Suspected Parasite

Please leave clear and concise information regarding your concerns, the location, missing person reports, unusual landscape or flora, and any suspected keeper sightings. If you think someone you know is harboring a keeper, or hiding a parasite's location, please contact your local BPR-PD. They will ask for your name, address, and the information you can give about the suspect(s). If they have any further questions, you will receive a follow-up email within 2 - 5 business days. Thank you for your help and cooperation.
